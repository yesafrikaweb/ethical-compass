The GRC Evolution

A detailed technical comparison between legacy GRC methodologies and the Ethical Compass active intelligence framework.

Analytical Method

Traditional GRC
Manual Assessment

Relies on periodic, human-led audits and subjective questionnaires. Subject to cognitive bias and significant administrative overhead.

Ethical Compass
Continuous Governance Intelligence

Automated, real-time analytical observation of data streams. Built on a persistent "Intelligence Framework" that removes human error from the initial identification phase.

Detection Latency

Traditional GRC
Quarterly / Annual

Risks are often only identified during formal audit cycles, creating a "Detection Gap" where failures can persist for months before being flagged.

Ethical Compass
Near Real-Time Oversight

Continuous monitoring ensures that ethical drift is identified the moment it occurs, allowing for immediate remediation and continuity assurance.

Data Sovereignty

Traditional GRC
Public Cloud Dependencies

Most modern GRC tools operate exclusively on public cloud infrastructures, creating potential data sovereignty and security risks for sensitive institutions.

Ethical Compass
Air-Gapped / Sovereign AI

Deployable as a self-contained, air-gapped enclave. All data processing remains within the physical and digital perimeter of the organization.
